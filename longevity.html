Mid-life predictors of Longevity

Data exploration

This study uses a well known database of 10058 men recruited in 1963 and were followed up for 5 years to investigate ischemic heart disease incidence (Medalie et al. 1973). The goal is to predict longevity using mid-life predictors.

Whats inside?

This data frame contains 135 variables:

  1. Demographics data (32)

  2. Medical data including medical history, laboratory tests and measurements (42)

  3. Daily physical activity assessment (4)

  4. Work related data (e.g. satisfaction, income etc.) (23)

  5. Family relations (10)

  6. Diet habits (23)

  7. Outcome - reaching to the age of 95, prevalence of 6.9 %

What are we going to do

  1. prepare our data for ML analysis

  2. create specifications for the different models

  3. hyper-tune parameters

  4. examine results

  update_role(id_nivdaki, new_role = "ID") %>%
  step_date(all_date_predictors(), keep_original_cols = FALSE, features = "month") %>%
  step_integer(all_ordered_predictors()) %>%

  step_zv(all_numeric_predictors()) %>%

xgb_wf <- workflow() %>%
